The Educational Funnel: Completion Rate Analysis
Evolution of the ratio between primary graduates and drop-outs

This visualization analyzes the primary education completion rate at three key temporal intervals (2006, 2013, 2023), offering a comparative breakdown between the student population that finishes the study cycle ("Completed") and those who drop out prematurely ("Incomplete").
The pattern analysis reveals a seemingly counter-intuitive evolution: between 2006 and 2023, the recorded completion percentage rose from 61% to 68%. Although this 7-percentage-point increase suggests an improvement in the system's internal efficiency, the estimated "drop-off" variable must be carefully observed, as it still stands at 32% in 2023.
From an analytical perspective, this indicates that nearly one-third of the school-age population fails to overcome the barrier of primary education. The persistence of such a high incompleteness rate, despite the positive trend, highlights a correlation with socio-economic barriers induced by the conflict: the demand for child labor and the degradation of school infrastructure act as noise variables in the learning process.
