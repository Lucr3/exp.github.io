The Spiral of Oblivion: A Radial Timeline of Attention
Mapping the episodic nature of global interest

This radial visualization challenges the classic linearity of time to expose the cyclical nature of indifference. The spiral structure allows us to compare the density of searches month by month over a period of more than twenty years, revealing seasonal patterns that would otherwise be invisible.
The color contrast is stark: the rare saturated sections isolate the few moments when Yemen made an impact on global consciousness, almost always coinciding with specific media events. The absolute predominance of light tones, however, graphically visualizes an uncomfortable reality: international attention is reactive and ephemeral, unable to sustain its gaze on the crisis once the spotlight of the news has faded.
