One Country, Different Prices: Yemen's Divided Economy
How the conflict fragmented the national market, creating unsustainable food-cost zones
1.0s
—

More than just monitoring price lists, this map visualizes the deep economic divide that runs through the country. The data traces the invisible boundaries of a fragmented market, where geography determines access to survival.
The areas highlighted in dark red identify the governorates where inflation has transformed basic necessities—such as flour and legumes—into luxury goods. The color gradient tells the story of two divergent economic systems: areas where the market is holding up and vast areas where currency devaluation and supply chain disruption have systematically eroded the population's purchasing power.
