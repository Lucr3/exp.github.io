The Volatility of Survival: A Statistical Breakdown
Analyzing price variance and market instability across essential commodities

This chart is best viewed on a larger screen.

The real story isn't the price: it's the chaos. Box plots shift focus from cost to unpredictability, exposing a fundamental breakdown in Yemen's markets. Some goods hold relatively steady. Fuel doesn't. Key sectors swing wildly with no apparent pattern.
The red peaks tell a stark picture: extreme disparities between regions. A liter of gasoline, a kilo of flour; the same goods cost radically different amounts depending on where you are and what's available that moment. It's the data-driven portrait of an economy where stability has collapsed into scarcity, leaving families unable to plan even a single meal ahead.
