The Volatility of Survival: A Statistical Breakdown
Analyzing price variance and market instability across essential commodities

This visualization shifts the focus from ‘how much it costs’ to ‘how unpredictable it is’. Box plots reveal the schizophrenia of the Yemeni market: while some goods maintain a relatively narrow price range, key sectors such as fuel show violent fluctuations.
The anomalous peaks highlighted in red tell a story of extreme disparity: the same liter of gasoline or kilo of flour can cost radically different amounts depending on the region and availability at the time. It is the statistical representation of an economy where stability has become the scarcest commodity, making it impossible for families to plan for their survival.
