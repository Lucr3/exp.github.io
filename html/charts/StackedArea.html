Analysis of the mortality rate for the population under 15 years of age shows a steady downward trend over the long term, from a critical value of 39.6% in 1968 to 4.7% in 2023. This reduction in slope reflects historical advances in primary care and immunization campaigns conducted before the collapse of infrastructure. However, the relevant insight emerges from observing the last segments of the historical series: the slowdown in the curve during the years of conflict (2014-2025) signals a saturation of health progress. In a data journalism ecosystem, this graph serves as an indicator of resilience: despite the war crisis, the maintenance of rates below historical lows is the result of direct humanitarian interventions, but the fragility of the data highlights the risk of a possible rebound in mortality in the absence of political stability.
