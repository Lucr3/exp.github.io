The Architecture of Silence: A Statistical View
Frequency distribution of global search interest scores

If the time spiral shows when the world looks at Yemen, this histogram quantifies how intensely it does so. The verdict of the data is clear: indifference is the default condition.
The strongly asymmetrical (left-skewed) distribution shows the disproportion between apathy and empathy. The overwhelming majority of the months analyzed are clustered on the left side of the graph, confined to minimal interest scores (range 10-40). Conversely, the almost total absence of bars on the right side shows that moments of high global attention are so rare that they constitute true statistical anomalies, exceptions in a decade of media silence.
