Global Attention

On the ground, this crisis never stops. But globally? Interest comes in waves—disconnected, erratic, completely out of step with what's actually happening. This section shifts our focus from Yemen itself to how the world perceives it. We've analyzed twenty years of Google Trends data to track global consciousness around the conflict.
What emerges is sobering. These visualizations expose something deeply unsettling: attention doesn't flow steadily. Instead, it spikes unpredictably and evaporates just as quickly. People wake up to Yemen momentarily, then turn away into silence.
The numbers confirm what the visualizations suggest—this pattern of 'serial forgetfulness' shows up everywhere. Most of the time, attention to Yemen sits at rock bottom. The uncomfortable reality? For the online world, Yemen's suffering isn't a sustained conversation. It barely registers, flickering briefly before vanishing into the noise.
