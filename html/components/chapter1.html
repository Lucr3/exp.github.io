War and Disasters


Data collected over eleven years outlines a story that extends far beyond the confines of the combat zones. This chapter seeks to disentangle the Yemen crisis into its constituent elements, visualizing two parallel realities.
At the top, time and space define the geography of the war: a fluid representation of how political violence has saturated the region. At the bottom, the focus shifts to the structural and human consequences that do not directly derive from weapons.
From cholera to tropical storms, the hierarchical breakdown of events reveals the impact of hostile nature on a collapsed infrastructure. This analytical framework is essential for understanding how, in this decade, the humanitarian emergency has been fueled by a lethal synergy between armed conflict and natural disasters.
