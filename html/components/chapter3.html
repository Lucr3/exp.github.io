Child Mortality

An analysis of Yemen's demographic trends paints a picture of a nation under constant pressure. Through the processing of historical datasets and long-term projections, a picture emerges of a profound divergence between birth and death rates. Understanding these dynamics is essential to contextualize the scale of the humanitarian crisis: a rapidly growing population is confronted with fragile infrastructure and limited access to essential resources, making the socio-economic system vulnerable to external shocks and prolonged conflict.
